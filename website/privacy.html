Privacy

Last updated: 2026-04-22

Pointless is designed so that your video never leaves your Mac. This page explains exactly what that means.

What happens to camera frames

  • Camera frames are captured via AVFoundation and handed to Apple's Vision framework for hand-pose detection — all on-device.
  • Frames are processed in memory and never written to disk.
  • Frames are never uploaded to any server — ours or anyone else's.
  • The app has no account system, no login, and no cloud storage.

What we collect

By default, nothing is sent off your Mac.

If you opt in to diagnostic reporting in a future update, Pointless may use Apple's MetricKit to send anonymized crash + performance reports. These are aggregated by Apple and contain no personal data.

Permissions we request

  • Camera — required to see your hand.
  • Accessibility — required to move the cursor and send clicks on your behalf. Pointless does not use Accessibility APIs to read screen contents or monitor other apps.

Updates

Pointless checks for updates via Sparkle from https://pointless.app/appcast.xml. The update check contains standard HTTP headers only (e.g. User-Agent, OS version).

Data deletion

There's nothing to delete on our side. To remove Pointless from your Mac, drag the app to the Trash.

Questions? Email support@pointless.app.
